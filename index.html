Student Outreach Overview

This course is designed to support and mentor students. The inclusion of certified graders frees you to spend more time on one-to-one outreach. Remember the Student Outreach principles from your OL 110 WWB Instructor Certification course.

Student Outreach generally falls into categories like:

  • Non-participating & Failing Students
  • Missing Assignments
  • Low Performing Students
  • Positive Outreach
  • Inspired Outreach

Best Practice: Use students' personal email in addition to Canvas Inbox for outreach if available. You can find personal emails via the Contact List in Canvas.

Tracking Your Outreach

Keep track of who you’ve messaged so you don’t duplicate efforts. You can use the Gradebook notes column or a spreadsheet.

Centrally Managed Graders

This course uses certified graders. Please do not adjust grades in the Gradebook. Contact the ACL or your supervisor if you disagree with any grade. Students can resubmit assignments 1-2 times by default, or more if you allow it for the entire class.

If potential academic dishonesty arises, graders will note it in their queue, and you will be notified. You then address the student accordingly.

Student Support & Escalation

Encourage students to use the Student Portal for common non-course-specific questions, or fill out the Escalations form if more help is needed.

Dates, Late Work & Extra Credit

All assignments open at the start of the course. Typically, weeks 1-4 have an Until Date 14 days after the due date; weeks 5-7 until Thursday of week 7. Late work is allowed during that window. No extra credit is offered in this course.

Every Week

Facilitate and coach students to find success. You won't be grading, so focus on monitoring the Gradebook, encouraging students, posting announcements, and messaging individuals. Most of your time will be spent on outreach.

  • Daily: Check email, respond to discussion/announcement questions.
  • Before the week begins: Review that week's notes, post an announcement, possibly work through the assignments yourself if you're new.
  • During the week: Complete outreach, participate in class discussions, ensure grading is up to date (contact your ACL if not).

Week 01

Announcements

Let students know it’s a 7-week course. Stress the importance of working early and often each week. Encourage them to share some personal introductions and set up all needed technology.

Outreach

  • Non-participating Students: Contact those who haven’t engaged at all. Remind them of the Day 8 auto-drop policy for WWB courses. Encourage them to drop if they don’t plan to continue.
  • Late Adds: Watch for late-adding students, welcome them, and answer any questions.
  • Inspired Outreach: Pray for guidance on who might need extra help; keep notes in Gradebook or a spreadsheet.

Announcement for W02: Post or schedule by Monday with any important reminders or highlights.

Week 02

Announcements

Review students’ performance on their initial assignments. Provide a broad update in the weekly announcement. You may share relevant articles or spiritual insights related to coursework.

Outreach
